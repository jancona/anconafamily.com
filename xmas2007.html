December, 2007


Dear Friends,

I sit here at my kitchen table. I can watch the birds at the feeders and the little snow on the ground. How come Christmas comes so fast and unexpectedly every year? Here is a fairly concise update on our lives and those we love.

I hit the big 50 in March and had asked Jim to take me to spring training for my birthday. We did go to Florida, staying a few nights with Jim’s Aunt Judy and Uncle John in St. Pete. Jim’s mother and Uncle Ed were there, so it was a fun time. We then headed down to Fort Myers area, staying on Sanibel Island. Saw Dice-K pitch his first American game. Enjoyed the warmth and the baseball, had tickets for four games.

Jim has dealt with health challenges this year, including an ER visit during our Florida trip and a second a couple days before Tom’s graduation. It was eventually diagnosed as pan “involving all” sinusitis. After many antibiotics, steroids and finally surgery in September, we continue to try to figure it out! In the midst of this he ended up with hepatitis from the medicines, which did resolve. I did learn how to use the power mower this summer and was proud of my grass mowing skills.

We traveled to San Francisco in May, Jim was at a conference and I had a ball. I would head out every day to tour the city and geocache too. I would come back and download pictures to show him my day. We did see Katia, who lives in Berkeley, quite a bit. I took her to Fisherman’s Wharf and she rode the cable cars for the first time. We went to Muir Woods and Point Reyes National Seashore with her on our last day. What a beautiful city and lovely area!

We then traveled to Chicago in June for Tom’s graduation. The attendees included my mom, Jim’s parents and Jo Louise, Jack’s wife. Graduation was a beautiful day. We did take the guests on a boat tour of the river and lake and spent time over the weekend doing tourist things. Tom has stayed in Chicago. He started work the day we left at the Univ. of Chicago Graduate School of Business. He is a program manager in the Polsky School of Entrepreneurship. Tom has enjoyed this, especially having no business background. He has a studio apartment in Hyde Park. I spent a weekend with him in September, doing a mother’s touch on things, as well as a White Sox baseball game and Greektown and caching. Tom was home for a long weekend at the beach in July and a week at Moosehead with us in August. He was also home for his and Jim’s bdays in October and Thanksgiving. We have a regular Southwest Airlines shuttle..so is it Manchester, Providence or Hartford this time??? He is happy, well, gainfully employed and has health insurance and dental. What more could parents want?

Mark is doing well. It is always an adventure with him. He is living in Worcester with his girlfriend, now fiancé, Michelle and Zach (2) and Isaiah (1). He is working full time at a factory in Holden, Michelle works nights at Dunkin Donuts. They plan to marry in April and are good parents to the boys. Jim and I took the two of them to the zoo this fall, it was fun but exhausting! Isaiah has stayed over a few times and is walking. We had them outside playing in the leaves when Tom was home in October, so much fun.

Jim’s company, Constant Contact went public this fall. He still commutes into Waltham and enjoys his work. I am still with UMass Memorial Hospice, per diem working 3 days a week. Now that tuitions are finished I can take a little more time off now and then. I still love to geocache, passed 700 in November. I am chairperson of the Commission for Women of the Diocese of Worcester. We had a women’s conference in November, had over 650 women come, it was phenomenal. I gave Jim season’s tickets to Holy Cross basketball for his birthday, so we have enjoyed going to games.

Bailey, Mark and Tobey’s oldest graduated from Conn College in May. He is teaching math at Brentwood HS, Nashville, TN. His girlfriend, Elizabeth is in graduate school at Vanderbilt. Leland is in his second year at Bowdoin. We saw a game in November at Babson and plan to get up north a few times after the holiday to watch hockey. Ross, their youngest is a freshman in high school. Jack and Jo Louise’s oldest Daniel is a senior in high school, Sam and Aviva are in elementary school. Jack is in middle school, he quit banking and is almost finished his masters in education. He is ‘student’ interning this year and loves it. Katherine and Michael have two high school students, Maggie was just in South Pacific, she made a very cute nurse! Sara, Peter and Kathleen’s oldest graduated from high school in June. We celebrated that with her as well as Mary Ann’s 75th birthday. Sara is attending the College of NJ, Ewing NJ just a short distance from their home. Emily is a junior. We are traveling to PA this weekend to finally see her dance in the Nutcracker. Can’t wait! Our parents are still glad to see us! Betty made it to the beach this summer, she still walks and bikes a little. Mary Ann plays bridge and does her aerobics. Jim and his brother went down this fall to help with yard work. I know his parents were happy and grateful to see their boys.

And of course the Red Sox won the World Series, life is good. We have pictures of graduation, family and travels at www.anconafamily.com/gallery please check them out! Well the letter turned out longer than I expected. Hoping this finds you well. Wishing you health and happiness in the New Year.

With love,
