Merry Christmas!

I’ve been putting off this letter because it’s so hard to boil down a year to one page or one letter. It’s been interesting. I just got a card today that talks about the best of times and the worst of times…  I can relate! Where to begin? First, the house looks great. We moved back in two days before Christmas last year. It all seems like a bad dream with a wonderful ending. We just put a Vermont Castings propane stove in the living room. It completes the room and is wonderfully warm and inviting. My hip is excellent. I should have done it years ago!

Tom finished off his St. John’s career in fine style. Graduation was a wonderful day for us all. He had many fine schools to choose from (Dartmouth not among them) and some very generous financial aid packages. After a great deal of thought (and prayer), Tom decided to attend the University of Chicago. In April he and Jim went out to a program for admitted students and he decided that it was the school for him. Tom was home for Thanksgiving and seemed very happy. It was obviously the right choice. He loves the school, his classes, his friends and the city. It’s only a two hour plane ride from Providence and not too expensive on Southwest. Chicago has a core curriculum that occupies most of the first two years, so he won’t choose a major for a while. At present he is considering international studies or political science. He is involved in Model UN and is looking forward to the St. John’s MUN club coming out for the annual conference in January. He volunteers at Doc Films, the student film society, which allows him to also attend movies for free in his spare time. He worked at Hoyt’s, now Regal Cinemas all summer and is coming back for Xmas vacation. Great job, took me to four or five movies. My favorite was Finding Nemo.

I left my job as Parish Nurse in May. With Tom attending college I needed to get back in the ‘real’ work force. I am working for UMass Memorial Hospice. I am a per-diem nurse and have worked consistently 3 to 4 days a week, plus on-call and some weekends. It’s been busy. I just recently got my own caseload as one of our nurses left and I offered to fill in until someone new is ready. The people I work with are great and I really like what I do. It is rewarding to help individuals and families during very difficult times. It’s as close to parish nursing as I can get right now. I just got back from Vermont where I hosted Koinonia’s annual parish nurse retreat. It was a beautiful time away with the new snow. I’d like to stay per-diem but don’t know what the future holds.

Jim just officially lost his job. The startup he worked for ran out of money. They were pursuing new financing up until very recently, but ultimately the deal never came together. He has been househusband for a bit and helping with things around here. It’s been a Godsend. He is now sending out emails and resumes to contacts and hopes to come up with something before too long. I can probably go on staff
